# Town Hall â€“ Newark AI Community Nonprofit

## Project Brief

**Concept:** A community-facing nonprofit that hosts free AI town halls, training sessions, and educational content for Newark residents with mixed technical backgrounds. Emphasizes accessibility, plain language, and building lasting community connections via Discord, events, and a content hub (blog/vlog).

**Goals:**
- Demystify AI for everyday people
- Provide free, relevant training (e.g., "AI for small business", "AI and privacy")
- Build engaged community (not just one-off attendees)
- Empower Newark residents to participate in AI conversation, not just consume it
- Create volunteer opportunities for NJIT students

## 1. Discovery & User Research

### Stakeholder Interviews Script (Simulated)

#### Interview 1: Newark Small Business Owner (No AI Background)
**Goal:** Understand business owner's perspective, needs, barriers

**Questions:**
1. What does "AI" mean to you? What questions do you have about it?
2. Are you using any AI tools in your business today? (Knowingly or unknowingly?)
3. What's your biggest fear about AI (privacy, job loss, cost)?
4. If there was a free workshop on "AI for small business", would you attend? Why/why not?
5. How would you prefer to learn? In-person, online, video?

**Sample Insights:**
- AI feels abstract; wants concrete examples ("How can *I* use this?")
- Skeptical of tech hype; needs proof it's worth time investment
- Privacy concerns are real; wants reassurance
- In-person workshops feel more trustworthy than online videos alone

---

#### Interview 2: Community Organizer / School Administrator
**Goal:** Understand institutional perspective, partnership potential

**Questions:**
1. What's the biggest skill gap you see in your community?
2. Would your organization host a free training event? What would that look like?
3. How do you reach people who aren't actively looking for training (the skeptics)?
4. What topics would resonate most? (Privacy, business applications, career prep, ethics?)
5. How do you measure success of a community program?

**Sample Insights:**
- Credibility matters; wants backing from trusted institutions (NJIT, schools)
- Marketing is hard; word-of-mouth and partnerships are key
- Hands-on + theory combo works better than lecture alone
- Post-event connection (Discord, follow-up emails) keeps momentum

---

#### Interview 3: Newark Resident (Tech-Curious, Low Literacy)
**Goal:** Understand everyday person's barriers and motivations

**Questions:**
1. What made you interested in attending an AI event?
2. What does "AI" mean in plain English?
3. What worries you about AI? What excites you?
4. Would you attend a follow-up Discord community? How would you use it?
5. What would make you refer a friend?

**Sample Insights:**
- Needs jargon-free explanations with relatable examples
- Worried about being "too old" or "not tech-savvy enough"
- Values peer learning (wants others at her skill level)
- Wants to connect with others and keep learning post-event

## 3. Design & Branding

### Brand Guide: Town Hall

#### Logo & Visual Identity
- **Concept:** Town hall building + speech bubble or hands/community
  - Simple, inclusive, accessible
  - Conveys public gathering, dialogue, community power
- **Wordmark:** "Town Hall" in clear, readable sans-serif
  - Alt: "Newark AI Town Hall" with city indicator

#### Color Palette
- **Primary:** `#1D4D6D` (deep blue) â€” trust, stability, government-like
- **Secondary:** `#E67E22` (warm orange) â€” approachable, energy, inclusivity
- **Accent:** `#27AE60` (green) â€” growth, forward movement, hope
- **Neutral:** `#2C3E50` (dark gray) â€” text, `#ECF0F1` (light gray) â€” backgrounds

#### Typography
- **Heading font:** "Mulish" or "Rubik" (accessible, friendly, easy to read)
  - Weights: 600 (bold), 700 (extra bold)
- **Body font:** "Open Sans" or "Mulish" (highly legible, accessibility-focused)
  - Weight: 400 (regular), 600 (semibold), 700 (bold)
- **Note:** Both should have good WCAG contrast ratios and be readable at smaller sizes (for older adults)

#### Tone of Voice
- **Welcoming & Accessible:** "AI is for everyone. Here's what you need to know."
- **Clear & Jargon-Free:** Explain concepts simply; define tech terms
- **Empowering:** "Your questions matter. Your privacy matters. Your voice matters."
- **Example copy:** "Free AI training for Newark residents. No tech background needed. Ask anything."

#### Visual Examples
- **Event card:** Large text, clear date/time/location, photo of past attendees (diverse)
- **Blog post preview:** Headline, short excerpt, "Easy read" label (5â€“10 min read time)
- **Button style:** Rounded, accessible color contrast, large hit target (mobile-friendly)
- **Accessibility notes:** Color + icon (not color alone); high contrast; sans-serif; readable at 14px+

## 4. Implementation

### Event Workflow

**Timeline: From event planning to follow-up**

1. **Planning (2 weeks before event)**
   - Team decides topic (based on community feedback)
   - Identify speaker/facilitator
   - Create event in Sanity (status: "draft")
   - Write plain-language description

2. **Promotion (10 days before)**
   - Create event page on website
   - Design event flyer (PDF + digital)
   - Post to Facebook, Discord #events
   - Email past attendees
   - Recruit volunteers (setup, registration table, post-event follow-up)

3. **Registration Opens (7 days before)**
   - Sanity event status: "scheduled"
   - Google Form registration goes live
   - Registration confirmation email sent to attendees

4. **Event Day**
   - Volunteers arrive early (setup)
   - Doors open 15 min early (social time, name tags)
   - Event runs (60â€“90 min: intro, content, Q&A, wrap-up)
   - Volunteers capture photos/video (with permission)
   - Attendees invited to Discord at end

5. **Post-Event (within 48 hours)**
   - Post-event email sent with:
     - Recording link (if video)
     - Slide deck download
     - Key takeaways summary
     - Next event announcement
     - Feedback survey link
     - Discord invite link (if not already shared)
   - Volunteer thank-you email
   - Content team creates blog post / social media summary
